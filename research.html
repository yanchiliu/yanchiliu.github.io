Research Interests


Data mining, business analytics, mobile intelligence, smart cities, recommender systems.


Research Overview

My general areas of research have been data mining and business analytics, particularly applying techniques from smart cities and recommender systems for urban business intelligence. More specifically, the focus of my research is utilizing big and heterogeneous data generated by diverse sources (e.g., sensors, devices, vehicles, stores, human) to address various challenges in business and city development (e.g., site selection and customer targeting), to discover and understand the relationship among residents, businesses, and urban environment, and to ultimately assist intelligent business decision-making. This is an emerging interdisciplinary research field at the intersection of data mining, artificial intelligence, business analytics, and urban planning, to promote successful and sustainable development of businesses and cities. My work has been published in top venues of data mining, including ACM KDD, IJCAI, IEEE ICDM, IEEE TCYB, ACM TKDD.

POI Recommendation
POI recommender systems serve for the purpose of improving customer satisfaction and quality of service. Existing approaches for POI recommendation have been mainly focused on exploiting the information about user preferences, social influence, and geographical influence. However, these approaches cannot handle the scenario where users are expecting to have POI recommendation for a specific time period. To this end, I proposed a unified recommender system called WWO to integrate the user interests and their evolving sequential preferences with temporal interval assessment. As a result, this system has the ability in handling the scenario where users are expecting to have POI recommendation for a specific time period, and the traditional POI recommender system can be treated as the special case of the proposed system by setting this time period long enough. Specifically, to quantify users’ sequential preferences, I considered the distributions of the temporal intervals between dependent POIs in the historical check-in sequences. Then, to estimate the distributions with only sparse observations, I developed the low-rank graph construction model, which identifies a set of bi-weighted graph bases so as to learn the static user preferences and the dynamic sequential preferences in a coherent way. A summary of the preliminary work has been published in the 2016 SIGKDD conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (KDD16). Moreover, I further studied personalized POI recommendation by incorporating the degree of temporal matching between users and POIs (ICDM16), extending the flat structure of POIs to a hierarchical structure (ICDM17), exploiting visual contents in geo-tagged photos for personalized tour recommendation (MM17), and considering the asymmetric transition between POIs with time-aware metric embedding (WWWJ).

POI Demand Modeling
POI demand modeling is an important issue in urban regions for business site selection. While some efforts have been made for the demand analysis of some specific POI categories, such as restaurants, it lacks systematic means to support POI demand modeling. To this end, I developed a systematic POI demand modeling framework, named Region POI Demand Identification (RPDI), to model POI demands by exploiting the daily needs of people identified from their large-scale mobility data. Specifically, I first partitioned the urban space into spatially differentiated neighborhood regions formed by many small local communities. Then, the daily activity patterns of people traveling in the city were extracted from human mobility data. Since the trip activities, even aggregated, are sparse and insufficient to directly identify the POI demands, especially for underdeveloped regions, I developed a latent factor model that integrates human mobility data, POI profiles, and demographic data to robustly model the POI demand of urban regions in a holistic way. In this model, POI preferences and supplies are used together with demographic features to estimate the POI demands simultaneously for all the urban regions interconnected in the city. Moreover, I also designed efficient algorithms to optimize the latent model for large-scale data. A summary of the preliminary work has been published in the 2017 SIGKDD conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining (KDD17).

Intelligent Transportation
I studied user travel behavior to improve bus routing of bus companies for improving rider experience. With the transaction records of taxicabs and bus riding, I optimized the utilization efficiency of public transportation services, by accurately predicting the real demand for public transportation. To improve the accuracy of the demand prediction, I first extracted significant features and then propose a spatially and functionally context-aware model, which outperforms the baselines significantly. I finally used graph-based routing optimization to attract the maximum number of bus riders. We proposed a localized transportation mode choice model, with which we can dynamically predict the bus travel demand for different bus routing. This model is then used for bus routing optimization which aims to convert as many people from private transportation to public transportation as possible. We conducted our method using the trajectories generated by large number of taxis and buses in 2012 in Beijing, and evaluated our results with the real bus routing of Beijing. A summary of the preliminary work has been published and selected as best paper candidate in ICDM14, and the extended version of this work has been invited for publication in Knowledge and Information Systems (KAIS).
